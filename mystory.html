My Story

From patient struggle to scientific discovery - the story behind our mission

How It All Started

Hi! I'm Natalia and I have adrenal insufficiency since 2023.

The "optimal" treatment wasn't working for me.

The brain fog and constant fatigue made me a stranger to myself. At first I thought it was exhaustion from working too much but no amount of rest worked. I couldn't think clearly or quickly, which meant I couldn't work or study.

Life stopped at my best moment, just when things were starting to go really well.

Desperate and without strength, I couldn't stop searching for a solution, even if it was at the speed of a lame turtle. First I went to dozens of doctors until I got my diagnosis, and then, using my academic background, I began reading hundreds of research articles to understand what was happening to me and why the treatment wasn't working.

I finally understood that my symptoms had already been studied by many endocrinologists. They had proposals to solve it: rigorous studies showed that cortisol pumps could mimic the body's natural rhythms and significantly improve quality of life... But... they weren't available to me.

Research with hydrocortisone pumps

The Discovery

Commercial drug infusion pumps cost... $3,000. $4,500. $6,500 dollars, and even if I could buy them, they didn't deliver medications with the rhythms required for cortisol. My heart sank. Not only were they extremely expensive, but they weren't available in my country.

But I never give up. I expanded my search and found an article about an $89 insulin pump that worked as well as the expensive ones and could be reprogrammed because it was "open source". The researchers had shared everything for free: bill of materials, assembly instructions, 3D print-ready design... and all without patents or barriers.

At that moment I saw it clearly: we had everything to build a solution.
