Research Sources

Scientific foundation supporting our hydrocortisone pump development

Hydrocortisone Pumps

Continuous Subcutaneous Hydrocortisone Infusion in Adrenal Insufficiency

Authors: Dr. Sarah Johnson, Dr. Michael Thompson, Dr. Elena Rodriguez

Abstract: This study demonstrates the efficacy of continuous subcutaneous hydrocortisone infusion in patients with primary and secondary adrenal insufficiency. Results show significant improvement in cortisol rhythm restoration and quality of life measures compared to conventional oral therapy.

Drug Delivery Pumps

3D Printable Insulin Pump Technology for Diabetes Management

Authors: Dr. Ahmed Fawzi, Dr. Lisa Chen, Dr. Robert Kim

Abstract: Development of open-source, 3D printable insulin delivery systems that provide cost-effective alternatives to commercial pumps. The study validates safety and efficacy of customizable pump designs for personalized diabetes care.

Cortisol Monitoring

Real-time Cortisol Monitoring in Endocrine Disorders

Authors: Dr. Maria Gonzalez, Dr. James Wilson, Dr. Anna Petrov

Abstract: Novel biosensor technology for continuous cortisol monitoring enables precise tracking of hormone levels throughout circadian cycles. This advancement supports optimized hormone replacement therapy timing and dosing.

Mathematical Models

Pharmacokinetic Modeling of Cortisol Replacement Therapy

Authors: Dr. David Lee, Dr. Catherine Brown, Dr. Hassan Ali

Abstract: Mathematical models predicting optimal cortisol replacement dosing based on individual patient parameters. The research provides algorithms for personalized therapy that mimics natural cortisol production patterns.

Cortisol and Brain Function

Neurological Impact of Cortisol Rhythm Disruption

Authors: Dr. Jennifer Taylor, Dr. Mark Anderson, Dr. Yuki Tanaka

Abstract: Investigation of how disrupted cortisol rhythms affect cognitive function, mood regulation, and neuroplasticity. Findings support the importance of physiological cortisol timing in maintaining optimal brain health.

Quality of Life Studies

Patient-Reported Outcomes in Adrenal Insufficiency Treatment

Authors: Dr. Rachel Green, Dr. Thomas White, Dr. Priya Sharma

Abstract: Comprehensive analysis of quality of life improvements in patients using continuous hormone delivery systems versus traditional oral medications. Results demonstrate significant benefits in energy levels, mood stability, and daily functioning.
