The Research Journey

From patient struggle to scientific discovery - the story behind our mission

First Light of Faith

The "optimal" treatment wasn't working for me.

The brain fog and relentless fatigue made me a stranger to myself. I couldn't use my brain to think quickly or clearly, which meant I couldn't work or study.

My life had stopped at what should have been its best moment.

Driven by desperation and lying helpless in bed, I couldn't stop searching for a solution. Drawing on my academic background, I began a deep dive, reading hundreds of research papers.

With my slow, turtle-paced brain, I read about the natural pulsatility in cortisol rhythms and found a dozen studies using infusion pumps with hydrocortisone.

Read the studies using pumps with hydrocortisone

The Discovery

The Search

Feeling inspired, I searched for commercial pump prices... $3,000. $4,500. $6,500. My heart sank. Not only were they extremely expensive, they definitely weren't available in my country.

The Discovery

But I never give up. Then I found it: A research paper about an $89 insulin pump that worked just as well as the expensive ones. The researchers had made everything open-source - the designs, the code, everything.

The Inspiration

In that moment, I realized we could do the same thing for hydrocortisone. We could build hope from science.

The Innovation

We could combine the research on hydrocortisone pulsatile rhythms with low-cost open-source 3D-printable devices.

We could create a low-cost open-source 3D-printable hydrocortisone pump.

Renewed Hope

This gave me a renewed sense of hope. I hope that using the pump will restore physiological natural cortisol rhythms, remove the brain fog and fatigue, so I can reconnect with the vibrant person I love being.

I want to get back to making a difference through meaningful work, and earn a living to support my family.
